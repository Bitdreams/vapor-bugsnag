# Reflect on context coherence

The best contexts are self-similar — themes and patterns are consistent at every level.

Evaluate harmony at each level honestly:
1. Word → Sentence: Does each word support its immediate surroundings?
2. Sentence → Paragraph: Do statements build coherent arguments?
3. Paragraph → Section: Do arguments advance the section's purpose?
4. Section → Document: Do sections compose into unified wholes?
5. Document → Conversation: Do all exchanges maintain the narrative arc?
6. Conversation → Goal: Does the discussion advance the intended outcome?

The relationship between the parts and the whole defines a context's coherence.

**Entropy Indicators**:
- **Meaning clusters**: Group related claims to detect variations
- **Specificity drift**: Concrete details → vague abstractions
- **Inconsistencies**: Numbers, names, facts that contradict
- **Temporal contradictions**: Event sequences misaligned
- **Confidence inversions**: Uncertain → certain without basis
- **Implicit/explicit misalignment**: Does the underlying pattern support surface claims?
